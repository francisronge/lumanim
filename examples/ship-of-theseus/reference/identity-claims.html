Reference card · Ship of Theseus

Two identity claims

Continuity

Claim: the same object persists through a connected history of gradual repair.

Favors: the continuously maintained ship.

Composition

Claim: the original matter determines which later object is the original.

Favors: the ship reassembled from the old planks.

The contradiction-shaped pressure

The two later ships are distinct. If each is identical to the one earlier ship, ordinary transitivity of identity creates a conflict. The thought experiment forces a theory to explain which relation matters—or why the question is framed wrongly.
