Reference card · Sorites

The harmless step
and the impossible march

1. Clear start

A sufficiently large collection is plainly a heap.

2. Tolerance premise

If n grains are a heap, removing one grain does not make them a non-heap.

3. Repetition

Apply that same adjacent-step judgment throughout the sequence.

4. Unacceptable endpoint

The argument eventually classifies one grain as a heap.

The exact pressure

You cannot preserve all three without explanation: clear endpoint cases, no decisive one-grain boundary, and unrestricted repetition of the tolerance premise.

Diagnostic question

When you meet a new Sorites-style argument, ask: Which apparently harmless local judgment is being repeated?
