# Paradoxes Resources

## Knowledge

- [Plutarch, *Life of Theseus*, 23.1 — Perseus Digital Library](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus%3Atext%3A2008.01.0067%3Achapter%3D23%3Asection%3D1)
  Primary ancient passage: the Athenians replaced decaying timbers and philosophers disputed whether the vessel remained the same.
- [“Identity Over Time” — Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/identity-time/)
  Scholarly account of the harder replacement-and-reassembly case, where two later ships each appear related to the original.
- [“Sorites Paradox” — Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/sorites-paradox/)
  Scholarly account of the paradox's inductive and line-drawing forms, its connection to vague predicates, and the major families of response. Use for: the structure and philosophical significance of Lesson 2.
- [“Eubulides” — Oxford Classical Dictionary](https://doi.org/10.1093/acrefore/9780199381135.013.2518)
  Classical reference on the fourth-century BCE dialectician traditionally credited with the Sorites and related puzzles. Use for: historical attribution only.
- [“Liar Paradox” — Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/liar-paradox/)
  Scholarly account of the simple-falsity Liar, Liar cycles, the principles used to derive contradiction, and major response families. Use for: the structure and philosophical significance of Lesson 3.
- [“Liar Paradox” — Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://iep.utm.edu/liar-paradox/)
  Accessible step-by-step derivation showing why assuming either truth or falsity forces the opposite value. Use for: checking the lesson's introductory reasoning.
